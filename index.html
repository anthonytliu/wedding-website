FAQs

What should I wear?

Please see “Dress” above. Feel free to send us any questions or outfit ideas!


Can I bring a plus one?

Due to the intimate and interactive nature of our wedding, we’ve selected venues that have limited capacity. Every guest who is invited will be explicitly listed on the invitation.


Gifts and Registry?

Your love and presence on our special day is truly the greatest gift we could ask for! If you feel compelled to give in the traditional sense, we’ve put together a registry here. Most of all, we’re just excited to celebrate with you!

For folks that appreciate a charitable donation option, please consider donating to these organizations on our behalf: Sister Song or Good Karma Bikes


Can I bring my children?

Similar to our answer to plus ones, only those who have invitations can attend.


Will the venue be outdoors or indoors?

The ceremony will be indoors. The cocktail hour will be on a backyard patio. The dinner will be indoors. The after party will be at a brewery with both indoor and outdoor seating. Bring layers!


Will there be parking?

No, unfortunately, none of the venues have accessible parking. If you are driving to the venues, we recommend budgeting a generous amount of time to find parking. If you opt for public transport, please account for potential delays. It’s very important to us that folks are on time to the ceremony (3:45pm sharp!) in particular.


What kind of food will be served?

We will be serving Filipino and Chinese-influenced New American cuisine with a focus on local, farm-to-table food. Please let us know of any dietary restrictions you may have so that you can enjoy the meal to its fullest.


Will there be alcohol?

Yes, we will have a small selection of alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages during the cocktail hour so that everyone can participate. If you elect to bring your own alcoholic beverage, you will be firmly asked to leave our gathering.


Do I have to attend the after party?

No – only warm invitation, no obligation.


What do you mean by “intimate and interactive” gathering?

We really want our community to learn more about one another and celebrate love. We appreciate the time, care, and intent you have all brought to the save-the-date questionnaire responses. We will use components of the questionnaire to create icebreaker-style materials for our ceremony, cocktail hour, and dinner. Please reach out if you are curious about specific ways we are planning to do this!


Can I take pictures during the wedding?

We really want everyone to be present in the moment. We’ll have a skilled and creative photographer (Anna Gillespie) and videographer (Emma Burke) documenting everything, so please keep phones and cameras away during the ceremony.

First Feeling of Love:

MM:

The first times I felt especially loved were around the age 3, thanks to my mom and Auntie Elma.

My mom would braid my hair every single morning before preschool; even then I realized that my requests to have PERFECTLY braided hair EVERY morning were pretty high maintenance. I remember thinking something along the lines of "wow she must love me a lot" as I carried my brush and hair ties to her at 7am.

Another early instance of feeling loved left marks on me (physically, a scar on my right thumb, and in my conscience too). It was a late summer evening, and we were picking blackberries at the end of the road. I was very excited at the idea of blackberry treats, and though my mom and Uncle Larry both warned me not to run home with my hands full, I ran, and inevitably tripped and slammed into the ground. I crushed a bowl full of blackberries and somehow tore open my right thumb; the juices mixed with my blood. Mom and Uncle Larry issued a (warranted) "I told you so" while I stomped the rest of the way home screaming.

Auntie Elma sat me on the bathroom counter and cleaned out my thumb, equal parts exasperated and worried. She calmed me by yelling "DEEP BREATHS" to cool my screaming. She bandaged me up so gently and patiently, more kindly than I felt I deserved, and I felt safe and loved.

AL:

This isn't the first time exactly, but it is the most vivid moment of my childhood. When I was 7, I became incredibly sick with a condition known as IPT where my white-blood cells were being destroyed by my spleen (autoimmune disorder). On Thanksgiving Day, my father drove me to the hospital in heavy snow (as we lived in Connecticut at the time) to the hospital to receive treatment. It was an intensive process with many weeks of blood transfusions, steroid boosters, and hospital food. Mid-way through my stay, my mother brought me the fifth Harry Potter book as it had just been released. During this time I couldn't do much except read and get well, but I have always cherished how loved I felt by both my parents during that difficult period of my life.
